Graduate Stories

Welcome to our graduate stories page, where we showcase the success stories of our Bachelor of Arts (Hons) in Culinary Arts graduates. Our program is designed to equip students with the skills and knowledge needed to establish a career in the culinary sector and beyond. We take great pride in our graduates who have gone on to pursue a variety of careers, from working as specialist chefs to founding their own food product innovation centers. These stories serve as inspiration to our current students and show the limitless potential of a degree in culinary arts.

1 year ago Culinary Arts

The skills and knowledge I gained from my degree program have been invaluable in my career.

Placement and Internship Stories

Welcome to our placement and internship stories page, where we highlight the experiences of our Bachelor of Arts (Hons) in Culinary Arts students during their placement and internship programs. Our program is designed to provide students with practical industry experience, and we are proud to partner with some of the leading restaurants and companies in Ireland. These stories showcase the hands-on learning opportunities available to our students and how they apply the skills and knowledge gained from the program in real-world settings. We hope these stories inspire our current and future students to pursue their passions in culinary arts and take advantage of the opportunities available to them.

Internship at Chapter One
Summer 2022 Chapter One Restaurant

As part of my culinary arts degree at SETU, I completed a summer internship at Chapter One, a Michelin-starred restaurant in Dublin. During my time there, I worked alongside the kitchen team to prepare and present dishes, and gained valuable experience in food preparation, plating and presentation. I also had the opportunity to work with some of Ireland's finest ingredients and learned about the importance of seasonality and sustainability in the culinary industry. Overall, it was an incredible experience and has helped me to develop my skills and passion for cooking.

Placement at Ballymaloe House
Spring 2022 Ballymaloe House

During the spring semester of my culinary arts degree, I had the opportunity to complete a placement at Ballymaloe House in County Cork. The experience was incredible and allowed me to work with some of the finest local ingredients and produce. Under the guidance of the head chef, I was able to learn about the principles of farm-to-table cooking and how to create dishes that showcase the natural flavors of the ingredients. I also had the chance to work alongside some incredibly talented chefs and developed skills in kitchen management and teamwork. Overall, the placement was a fantastic experience and has helped me to develop my skills and knowledge in the culinary arts.

Internship at Forest and Marcy
Summer 2021 Forest and Marcy

As part of my culinary arts degree at SETU, I completed a summer internship at Forest and Marcy, a popular restaurant in Dublin known for its innovative cuisine. During my time there, I had the opportunity to work with some incredibly talented chefs and gained valuable experience in creating unique dishes and experimenting with different flavor combinations. I also learned about the importance of presentation and how to create visually stunning dishes that are as beautiful as they are delicious. Overall, the internship was an incredible experience and has helped me to develop my skills and passion for cooking.

Placement at The Pig's Ear
Fall 2021 The Pig's Ear

During the fall semester of my culinary arts degree, I completed a placement at The Pig's Ear, a popular restaurant in Dublin known for its modern Irish cuisine. During my time there, I had the opportunity to work with some incredibly talented chefs and gained valuable experience in creating dishes that celebrate Ireland's rich culinary traditions. I also learned about the importance of sourcing ingredients locally and seasonally, and how to create dishes that are sustainable and environmentally friendly. Overall, the placement was an incredible experience and has helped me to develop my skills and knowledge in the culinary arts.



Erasmus Stories

At the South East Technological University, we believe that a well-rounded culinary education should extend beyond the classroom and into the wider world. That's why we offer our Bachelor of Arts (Hons) in Culinary Arts students the opportunity to study abroad and gain invaluable international experience. Our students have the chance to expand their culinary knowledge, immerse themselves in new cultures, and network with industry professionals from around the globe. Whether it's through our academic study abroad programs or our international internship placements, studying abroad with SETU is a transformative experience that enhances our students' education and sets them up for success in the global culinary industry.

Discovering New Culinary Techniques in Spain
Erasmus Year 2 Barcelona, Spain

During my Erasmus year in Barcelona, I had the opportunity to work in some of the city's best restaurants and learn new culinary techniques from local chefs. I was also able to immerse myself in the local food culture and explore the city's markets and food festivals. It was an unforgettable experience that has greatly influenced my career in the culinary arts.

Adapting to New Ingredients and Flavors in Italy
Erasmus Year 3 Florence, Italy

My Erasmus year in Florence allowed me to work with ingredients and flavors that were completely new to me. I had to adapt quickly to the local cuisine and learn to work with traditional Italian ingredients like truffles and prosciutto. It was a challenging but rewarding experience that has broadened my culinary horizons.

Exploring Food Sustainability in the Netherlands
Erasmus Year 4 Amsterdam, Netherlands

My Erasmus year in Amsterdam focused on food sustainability and the impact of the food industry on the environment. I had the opportunity to work with local organizations and learn about sustainable food production and distribution. This experience has had a lasting impact on my approach to food and has inspired me to promote sustainable practices in my culinary career.

Learning New Techniques and Flavors in Greece
Erasmus Year 2 Athens, Greece

My Erasmus year in Athens allowed me to work with traditional Greek ingredients and learn new culinary techniques from local chefs. I was also able to explore the city's markets and discover new flavors and ingredients. It was an enriching experience that has broadened my culinary repertoire and inspired me to continue learning and experimenting with new flavors.
